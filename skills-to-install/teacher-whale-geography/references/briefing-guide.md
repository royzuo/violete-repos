# Geography Briefing Guide

Use this guide when resolving a geography section or wonder topic and structuring the bundle.

## Scope resolution

Prefer a section that already exists in the program outline or briefing file. If Roy has not pinned a section yet, use the current in-repo briefing source `prompts/geography_briefing.txt` to infer the topic direction.

For the current geography direction, `水的史诗` is the active theme signal in the repo prompt, but do not hardcode it as the only future theme.

Selection rules:

- The topic can be natural geography, human geography, or a combined human-nature product.
- It must be distinctive and influential.
- It must be strongly relevant to geography as a science.
- If there is important news within the last three days, prefer a topic with fresh news.
- If possible, prefer wonders that include unresolved mysteries or open scientific questions.
- Avoid repeating wonders that have already been covered.

## Briefing-card structure

The content should usually map to 5 to 8 cards. A common geography pattern is:

1. Real question and hook
2. Location and system setting
3. Formation process or spatial mechanism
4. Human-earth interaction or geographic significance
5. Current challenge, comparison, or unresolved issue
6. Conclusion and future

Use fewer or more cards when the section needs it.

## Content requirements

- Background and value:
  Explain the wonder and why it matters in earth science, ecology, and human civilization.
- Geographic location:
  Explain where it is, what surrounds it, and how geology, ecology, and human life connect there.
- Formation process:
  Cover tectonics, erosion, deposition, hydrology, or other key processes, plus human adaptation, engineering, or land use.
- Geographic significance:
  Explain how geography shapes culture and economy. Integrate recent news and unresolved mysteries here when appropriate.
- Current status, challenges, and sustainable future:
  Explain protection status, threats, conservation work, governance, and sustainable use.

## Interaction requirements

- Add an interaction prompt in the first part.
- Add another interaction prompt near the ending.
- Invite the audience to reply in comments with opinions, guesses, or answers.

## Talkshow requirements

- The talkshow script should stay within about 3 minutes.
- Keep the spoken style lively, but do not introduce unsupported facts.

## Video prompt requirements

- Prompt text must be in English only.
- Each clip must be 15 seconds or less.
- Produce one clip per card in most cases.
- Follow the pattern `camera + subject + action + atmosphere + lighting + concise quality tags`.
- Prefer concrete camera instructions such as pan, push-in, pull-back, aerial, follow, static shot, close-up, or handheld.
- Make each clip correspond to the matching card's explanatory job rather than the overall topic.
